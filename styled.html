The American Revolution

"no taxation without representation"
Over time, the colonies had grown functionally independent from the British crown. Great distance/ocean separated the colonies from motherland. Free market ideas/capitalism opposed European mercantilism Colonists had no representation in British parliament but continued to be heavily taxed.

The French Revolution

"liberté, egalité, fraternité"
The commoners were severely underrepresented in the Estates-General; formation of the National Assembly. Riots forced Louis XVI to arrange a more prominent role for the National Assembly. Louis XVI's opposition to limited monarchy culminated in his beheading.

The Haitian Revolution

"the rich are only defeated when running for their lives"
Drew inspiration from the French Revolution. The enslaved majority rose up against their few French masters. Toussaint L’ouverture took charge. Haitians were victorious against the French. Established an independent Haitian government
